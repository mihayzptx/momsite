What's Happening This Week

Your baby is putting on weight and developing more fat. Their lungs are maturing and preparing to breathe air after birth. Their eyes can now open and close, and they're developing a sense of sight. The brain is growing rapidly, forming billions of neurons. Your baby might be settling into a sleeping pattern, with periods of wakefulness and sleep.

Your Body at Week 28

You're now in your third trimester! Your uterus has expanded significantly and is likely putting pressure on your organs, which can cause some discomfort. You may notice more Braxton Hicks contractions as your body prepares for labor. Your growing baby might be causing backaches and making it harder to find a comfortable sleeping position.

Common Symptoms at 28 Weeks

Shortness of Breath
Your expanding uterus is pressing on your diaphragm, making it harder to take deep breaths.
Backaches
The weight of your growing baby puts strain on your back muscles.
Leg Cramps
You may experience more cramping in your calves, especially at night.
Braxton Hicks
These practice contractions help prepare your body for labor.

Nutrition Tips for Week 28

Your baby is growing rapidly now, and your nutritional needs are higher than ever. Focus on protein, iron, calcium, and omega-3 fatty acids. Stay hydrated and continue taking your prenatal vitamins.

Increase Iron Intake
Iron-deficiency anemia is common during pregnancy. Eat iron-rich foods like lean red meat, spinach, and beans. Pair with vitamin C to enhance absorption.
Omega-3 Fatty Acids
Essential for your baby's brain development. Include sources like salmon, chia seeds, and walnuts in your diet.
Stay Hydrated
Proper hydration helps prevent constipation, urinary tract infections, and preterm labor. Aim for 8-10 glasses of water daily.

Your 28-Week Checklist

As you enter your third trimester, there are some important tasks to consider for preparing for your baby's arrival.

Start Your Birth Plan
Begin thinking about your preferences for labor and delivery. Discuss options with your healthcare provider and partner.
Register for Childbirth Classes
Sign up for childbirth education classes. They typically last 6-8 weeks, so now is a good time to start.
Research Pediatricians
Begin looking for a pediatrician for your baby. Ask for recommendations and schedule interviews.
Start Baby Shopping
Begin purchasing essential items for your baby. Don't forget to use our Discount Map to save on your purchases!
